Siddhpeeth
Mahavir Mandir
5 AM - 11 PM
Near Junction

One of the holiest Hanuman temples in North India, famous for its 'Naivedyam' sweets and miraculous aura...

Mahavir Mandir is one of the most prominent temples dedicated to Lord Hanuman. It is visited by millions of devotees every year. The temple is world-renowned for its 'Naivedyam' laddoos prepared by experts from Tirupati.

Highlights:

  • Floating stones from Ram Setu preserved here
  • Dual idols of Lord Hanuman (Sankat Mochan & Phaleron)
  • Significant contribution to social service through cancer hospitals

Pro Tip: Visit during the early morning 'Aarti' for a soul-stirring experience.

Legacy of Empires

The Pataliputra Timeline

2,500 years of power, wisdom, and revolution condensed into a journey

490 BCE

Foundation of Pataligrama

King Ajatashatru builds a fort near the confluence of the Ganges and Son rivers, marking the birth of a world capital.

321 BCE

The Mauryan Peak

Chandragupta Maurya and Ashoka the Great transform Pataliputra into one of the largest cities in the ancient world.

320 CE

Gupta Golden Age

The city becomes a hub of science and art. Aryabhata records astronomical marvels while teaching at Nalanda nearby.

1541 CE

Sher Shah Suri Arrival

The great Afghan ruler revives the city, renames it 'Patna', and builds the iconic mosque that still stands today.

1786 CE

The Colonial Era

The British East India Company builds the Golghar. Patna becomes a vital trade hub for opium and indigo.

2026 CE

Patna Modernity

A vibrant metropolis blending skyscrapers with stupas, ready to welcome the world with digital heritage tools.

Flavor Fusion

The Bihari Kitchen

Embark on a culinary voyage through the rustic and refined tastes of Patna.

Bihari Soul
The Classic
Litti Chokha
Coal Roasted
Pure Ghee

Roasted wheat balls stuffed with spiced sattu (gram flour), served with smoky eggplant mash...

The culinary identity of Bihar, Litti Chokha is a rustic masterpiece traditionally baked over dried cow-dung cakes for a unique smoky flavor.

The Experience:

  • Litti: Whole wheat dough filled with 'Sattu' (roasted gram flour)
  • Chokha: A spicy mash of roasted eggplant, tomatoes, and potatoes
  • Served with a generous bowl of yellow clarified butter (Ghee)
  • Best enjoyed with raw onions and traditional green chutney
Royal Sweet
Silao Special
Silao Khaja
52 Layers
GI Tagged

A crisp, multi-layered delicacy that melts in your mouth, traditionally associated with Buddhist history...

A Geographical Indication (GI) tagged sweet from Silao, this treat consists of multiple thin sheets of dough layered and deep-fried.

Why it's Special:

  • Incredibly crisp yet light texture
  • Slightly sweet with a hint of cardamom
  • Roots dating back to the Mauryan empire
  • Perfect souvenir for travelers
Street Legend
Morning Fuel
Chana Ghugni
Spiced
With Poha

Spicy black chickpea curry flavored with ginger and local chillies, a favorite breakfast across Bihar...

A protein-packed, spicy delight made from black chickpeas (Kala Chana), often served with 'Choora' (pounded rice) or 'Kachri'.

Taste Profile:

  • Thick, aromatic gravy with cumin and garam masala
  • Topped with chopped onions, green chillies, and lemon juice
  • A staple during the holy month of Ramadan for Iftar
  • Available at every street corner in the mornings
Festival Special
Sweet Pancake
Bihari Malpua
With Rabri
Holi Essential

Fluffy, deep-fried pancakes soaked in sugar syrup, flavored with fennel and loaded with dry fruits...

Unlike the dry versions elsewhere, Bihari Malpua is traditionally dipped in thick sugar syrup (Chasni) and is an absolute must-have during Holi.

The Recipe:

  • Batter made of flour, milk, mashed bananas, and sugar
  • Infused with fennel seeds (Saunf) for a refreshing aroma
  • Fried until the edges are golden and crisp
  • Best served with a side of cold, creamy Rabri
Winter Classic
Gaya Specialty
Gaya Tilkut
Seasonal
Sesame Base

Crunchy, pounded sesame and jaggery treats, handcrafted with precision for the perfect snap...

A winter staple, Tilkut from Gaya is famous for its incredibly light and crisp texture, achieved by manual pounding of sesame and jaggery.

Winter Traditions:

  • Hand-pounded over iron mortars for hours
  • Made with high-quality sesame seeds (Til)
  • Consumed primarily during Makar Sankranti
  • Available in sugar (Cheni) and jaggery (Gur) varieties
Traditional Dimsum
Rice Flour Dumpling
Bihari Dal Pitha
Steamed
Vegetarian

The 'Bihari Momo'—steamed rice flour shells stuffed with spicy lentil paste, served with tangy chutney...

Often called the 'Bihari Momo', Dal Pitha is a healthy, steamed dumpling made from rice flour and stuffed with spiced Chana Dal paste.

Healthy Heritage:

  • Steamed, making it a low-fat, nutritious snack
  • Filling seasoned with ginger, green chillies, and garlic
  • Traditionally shaped into crescents or rounds
  • Served hot with spicy tomato chutney or fried for extra crunch
Holy Offering
Chhath Special
Traditional Thekua
Whole Wheat
Sacred Prasada

The signature 'cookie' of Bihar, deep-fried whole wheat biscuits flavored with fennel and cardamom...

The primary 'Maha Prasada' of the Chhath festival, Thekua is a dense, crunchy biscuit made from wheat flour, jaggery, and dry coconut.

Cultural Significance:

  • Traditionally made using hand-carved wooden molds
  • Deep-fried in pure desi ghee for an irresistible aroma
  • Symbolizes the simplicity and health of rural Bihari diet
  • Can be preserved for weeks without losing its crunch
Sweet Crunch
Sesame Coated
Patna Anarsa
Sesame Rice
Rice Based

A unique rice-based sweet coated in white sesame seeds, offering a perfect balance of soft and crunchy...

Anarsa is an intricate sweet made from fermented rice flour and jaggery, meticulously shaped and coated with poppy or sesame seeds.

Craftsmanship:

  • Requires days of rice soaking and flour preparation
  • The sesame coating provides a nutty crunch to every bite
  • Often filled with 'Khoya' (milk solids) for richness
  • A staple during the festive season in Bihar
Legacy of Faith

Cultural Vibrance

Step into a world of timeless traditions and spectacular communal gatherings.

Maha Parv
Sun Worship
Chhath Puja
4 Days
Sacred River

Bihar's most revered festival where the city unites at the Ganges bank to worship the rising and setting sun...

Chhath is not just a festival; it's a divine emotion for every Bihari. It's the only Vedic festival dedicated to both the setting and rising Sun God.

The Rituals:

  • Nahay Khay: First day of purification
  • Kharna: Second day of fasting and sweet offerings
  • Sandhya Arghya: Third day of river offerings at sunset
  • Usha Arghya: Final day of sunrise offerings
